Why use Bower Tabletop Tiles?

The Story

The Cavern

You and your party of seasoned adventurers are in the middle of a dark cave. You have been traveling for days now and it seems that there is no end to the twists and turns of these cold dark tunnels. You think that this must be the cave that the old wizard told you about at the Blue Birch Tavern. Thinking about the occasion with the peculiar man, you remember something that did not seem that strange at the time, but now has been haunting your thoughts. He said, “In the dark of the cavern, I had to make a choice. A choice which has cursed me my whole life... even in my old age.” You think to yourself, “What was that choice? Must I make the same choice?”

Discovery

After about an hour of brooding over that conversation with the old wizard, Rezzing, an Elven Ranger that has been by your side for many adventures, speaks up, “What is that!?” You and your comrades look over in his direction, and to your surprise you see a small symbol, about the size of your palm, etched on the wall. That is the first sign of intelligent life you’ve seen in three days. At least you think it’s been three days… The group examines the small engraving on the wall. Fen’inu, the Wizard, says, “I think I have seen this symbol once before. It was in the Book of Shadow Magic.”
“You’ve read the Cursed Tome?” interjects Rezzing.
“Yes, it was part of my studies in the land of Fayh’leh. As I was saying, the Book of Shadow Magic said that it is a spell that connects to the earth. It should be harmless as long as we do not touch it.”
“Oh dear...” says Rezzing as his face turns white with fear, “I touched the symbol right before I told you about it.”
“What?! No!” Fayh’leh replies looking frantic.

Danger

Before another second passes by, the earth beneath you, like a living creature, reels back, and you and your comrades fall into the dark below... The fall was not too far and you did not get too badly hurt. You look around and notice, to your shock, that there are lanterns on the walls of this large room. There are doors on the far sides of the room. As everyone struggles to get their bearings, you notice, to your shock, that the doors are opening. You cry out to your companions, but before they understand what’s happening, two groups of six armed men charge out of each of the doors. You draw your sword and charge forward attacking the first one.
“Nope, you can’t do that!” says Mike, the Dungeon Master. He continues, “These guys are sixty feet away, so you can’t cover that distance and make an attack in your turn.”
Instead of charging forward, you help Fen’inu to his feet and take your shield out of your pack. You ready yourself for battle. You notice that your foe is taking on a strange battle formation, that you haven’t seen before. “Okay,” you tell Mike, “you can’t just say that it’s a strange battle formation that I’ve never seen before. Give me some details.”
“Oh, right. It's an S shape.” Mike replies, as he begins drawing on a piece of paper. “Like this.”
“That's a Z shape. How big is it? How far apart are they spaced? Where's the front of the line?”
“Uhhh...”
